Hi there! I’m Hanna Kurian.

I’m a User Experience designer with a dual background in architecture and engineering. I have always been an idea person. I’m always thinking of ideas that break open the mold of how something currently works - how can I better it? Will it work more efficiently if it is done this way? “There’s got to be a better way to do this” is always how my mind goes. Of course, applying theory I learnt in mathematics to real life problems was my favorite part of school. I know, such a nerd! Well, always been happy to be one! I discovered User Design as a field that can be pursued as a career option during my graduate program when I took a “service design” elective during my last semester. I thoroughly enjoyed the course and looked into my options of how I could segue my existing design career into the digital space. This brought me to Bloc where I honed my existing design skills that I acquired during my architecture career, coding skills from my engineering program and learned new ones that make me the better UX designer I am today. My dual background in engineering and architecture have always given me a unique vantage point where I look at problems both precisely and creatively at the same time. However, I believe my main strength is my empathy that keeps any design solution focused on the user from the beginning.

What introduced you to design?

I was introduced to the world of service design design through a class I took during my graduate program in lighting design. Being a habitual problem solver, defining what I had already been doing made me realize how passionate I am about good design. Both in my architectural and lighting design projects, users’ needs were always paramount. I realized that I had been doing user-centered design all along and I wanted to learn further and diversify the platforms for my user design into the digital world.

Why is design important to you?

I have always believed that good design is not evident till it’s missing. As a designer, I want to change the way the world thinks of design. There is design in everything. I aspire to make design a part of the process and not an afterthought.

How can you use design to make a difference?

During my career as a lighting designer I used the UX design process to redesign the existing website UI for the company I was working forThe previous website consisted of three pages that contained project images and list mentioning previous work, but nothing was linked to the other. I saw the opportunity for better design and took the initiative to revamp the entire website. I worked with the principal of the company to narrow down the best projects as well as create a sitemap to categorize projects that would create intuitive user flows for the respective clients. The resulting website had a clean, minimalist UI which showcased the best projects and piqued great user interest. The new website design generated more business for the company. As a designer, I believe I created a platform to successfully showcase years of great work to potential clients and generate more revenue through good design.
