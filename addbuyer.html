PROBLEM

To initiate the Early Pay app, suppliers commence by requesting buyer linkages, with 80% of linkages being manually executed. Regrettably, this crucial step suffers from a subpar user experience, lacking fundamental UX elements such as a search bar, filter capabilities, and contextual feedback, contributing to an overall poor user experience.

Furthermore, the process encounters challenges in efficient data utilization. Although the front-end appears to collect out-of-network requests, this valuable information is neither stored nor intelligently employed internally. This failure to leverage high-potential data not only hampers the expansion of the existing network but also represents a missed opportunity for revenue generation through other lending options within the company.

In summary, the deficiencies in user experience, coupled with the oversight in utilizing critical data, highlight significant opportunities for improvement in the overall process of requesting buyer linkages through the Early Pay app.

SOLUTION

To initiate a transformative improvement, the user experience requires a substantial upgrade by incorporating fundamental UI capabilities. Enhancing trust and communication can be achieved through the integration of Salesforce into the app. This integration will establish a seamless two-way communication channel between the backend and frontend, effectively reducing the existing inefficiencies.

A pivotal aspect of the solution involves implementing a robust data design to systematically collect out-of-network requests. Additionally, creating an internal dashboard accessible to key stakeholders will facilitate efficient utilization of this valuable data. This strategic approach ensures that various internal teams involved in the process can access and leverage the information effectively.

To gauge the effectiveness of the revamp, it is essential to implement a comprehensive tracking system for page traffic. Comparing metrics before and after the upgrade will provide a holistic perspective on the return on investment (ROI) for this effort. This data-driven approach will not only quantify the impact of the improvements but also guide future enhancements based on actionable insights.

DISCOVERY LIMITATIONS

"Where do we start? Where are the Subject Matter Experts?"



Legacy APIs: This feature has persisted from the outdated system the company transitioned away from four years ago. Consequently, the engineering team faced challenges in providing necessary information due to an incomplete understanding of the legacy codebase. This difficulty was exacerbated by the departure of key team members who had originally worked on it.

Missing Product Owner: This specific page finds itself in a state of ambiguity between two products within the app, lacking a clear line of product ownership. This absence has made the task of tracking down information and identifying a stakeholder an immensely challenging endeavor. It's akin to playing a game of hot potato, with each attempt to secure crucial details resembling a delicate yet elusive prize.

Lack of Priority (on the Product Delivery Roadmap):Due to the absence of a dedicated product owner, this page remains conspicuously absent from the product roadmap. Despite the company's persistent emphasis on converting single-buyer suppliers to multi-buyer suppliers and the associated revenue implications, there appears to be a collective oversight regarding the critical role this singular page plays in enhancing system efficiency and yielding greater output.

RESEARCH & DISCOVERY

PAGE TRAFFIC ANALYSIS

The page traffic analysis revealed a substantial volume of visitors, averaging at 41 users per day as a conservative estimate. However, when optimizing for a broader range of possibilities, it was observed that 61 out of 100 users visiting the page left without taking any action, underscoring significant issues with the UI and UX of the page.

Data Set: Total Visitors: 1830, Time period: 19th September, 2022 - 19th October 2022
The two charts plot the page traffic behavior if there were no overlap in actions taken (above) vs. if there was complete overlap (below)

This high percentage of users not engaging with the page highlighted missed opportunities. To further understand the impact, we can map these missed opportunities to the various potential paths the users could have taken to generate company revenue. This analysis reveals areas of concern in the user journey and identify opportunities for improvement in both design and user experience.

MAKING A BUSINESS CASE: METRICS

Acquiring the page traffic data marked just the initial phase of our research journey. It took a couple of attempts and formula iterations to discern the method for calculating projections derived from the prevalent missed opportunities on this page.

The data collected on this page stands to immensely benefit various stakeholders, notably the sales team responsible for onboarding buyers to the C2FO platform. Collaborating closely with a leader, we developed a formula (outlined below) to translate the number of customer requests into actual subscription fees, conservatively estimated at approximately $1 million.

An equally, if not more, significant outcome of this experience upgrade is the heightened frequency of successful linkages between suppliers and buyers. For each successful linkage, we conservatively projected an increase in Accounts Payable (A/P) flowing through the app. This, in turn, resulted in a conservative estimated company revenue of $2.27 million per year.

CURRENT STATE

Suppliers predominantly utilize this page to initiate a linking process with their buyer within the C2FO Ecosystem. Notably, while 20% of linkings occur automatically, a substantial 80% necessitate suppliers to visit this page to request linkage, a vital step for leveraging the early invoice payment application. This process isn't a one-time occurrence; suppliers must repeat it each time they seek linkage to another buyer within our ecosystem. Therefore, it constitutes an ongoing experience, the culmination of which directly translates into tangible revenue for the company.

Furthermore, this iterative linking process contributes to improved customer experiences for suppliers and enhances user retention. The revenue implications are significant, as the transition from a single-buyer-supplier to a multi-buyer-supplier results in a fourfold increase, according to company data. This singular metric underscores the importance of investing in and optimizing this experience, emphasizing its critical role in driving revenue growth and ensuring a positive and lasting impact on both customer satisfaction and user retention.

FRONT-END USER INTERFACE

The current user interface offers functionality for two core actions: initiating a new customer request and/or requesting an in-network division linking. However, a prominently featured element on the page, resembling a call-to-action button, is a misleading 'step tracker.' This element has proven to be a source of significant user frustration, particularly for first-time visitors to the page.

As highlighted earlier, essential UI interaction elements, including a search bar and contextual feedback, are conspicuously absent. This absence not only encourages the submission of duplicate requests but also erodes user trust. Users may be inclined to abandon the page without completing any actions due to the lack of clear guidance and an intuitive interface. Addressing these issues is crucial for enhancing the overall user experience and improving the page's effectiveness in facilitating user actions.

BACK-END: SUPPORT TEAM PROCESS

Designing a white-labeled product has its own set of rules and limitations when it comes to user interface designs and patterns. One of the first business rules that was non-negotiable was that the client logo needs to be persistent on the header of all screens. The only exception to this rule was in the transaction flows.

Another aspect of white-labeling is that prospective clients are shown their respective client branded products (seen below: Ivy Investments, Janus Henderson, Transamerica) so as to entice them to purchase the products. Further customizations are also possible depending on the client tiers and the add-ons they wish to add to their product.

THE PROPOSAL

FRONT-END: LOW-FIDELITY MOCKUPS

The low-fidelity mockups successfully addressed some of the fundamental UI elements currently lacking in the system. These improvements encompassed the introduction of a search bar and contextual feedback when initiating a request. To mitigate the occurrence of duplicate requests, we implemented a feature displaying the history of all requests within the existing customer dashboard.

For later phases of the project, we proposed more sophisticated solutions. These include recommendations for automatic/smart linking, leveraging the wealth of data within the systems. Additionally, we suggested incorporating upvotes to encourage community participation and intelligent suggestions based on historical patterns, providing a more advanced and user-friendly experience.

Explorations to see where the search bar would work better in the user flow.

Incorporation of contextual feedback in a linking request.


Automatic Linking for existing in-network buyers/suppliers when new ones get onboarded could reduce manual linking requests and increase overall process efficiency.

Upvotes for out-of-network buyers introduces a social, community aspect that is lacking on the platform today. This also reduces friction by making it fast and easy to request (one-click), resulting in more votes/frequency of requests.

Smart Suggestions of possible buyers from historical patterns of suppliers in the same markets will introduce intelligence into the system and increase linkages within network.

BACK-END: IMPROVED PROCESS FLOW

In collaboration with the support team lead, we worked to create a solution that automates checks in the support team's ticket handling flow. For example, if specific information was missing, the system would automatically send an email to the user requesting that information, eliminating the need for human intervention in the process flow. Through iterative refinement, we were able to propose a more efficient process workflow that enhances the support team's efficiency by 3x. This translates to reducing the need for human intervention in 1 out of every 4 instances, compared to its current state.

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPES

The initial high-fidelity mockups were completed within a week and done in tandem with the development during the company-wide hack week event. Remarkably, this project earned the prestigious 'Best in Show' title, securing the highest score across all categories. An impressive 80% of the primary front-end development occurred during this intensive week, showcasing the collaborative efforts of our stellar team comprising engineers, the sales team, and design professionals. The project garnered significant momentum and was subsequently transitioned to the team assigned to its ongoing development.

Following a comprehensive review by the extended design team, a second iteration of the designs was produced. This phase encompassed the incorporation of error states, empty states, and consideration for various scenarios. The refined designs were then handed off to the engineers as the Phase 1 Design Deliverable.

CONCLUSION

What initially seemed like an insurmountable challenge has turned into one of the most fulfilling experiences in my design career. Here's a non-exhaustive list of lessons learned from this project:

  • Taking ownership of a product
  • Pitching to leadership and succeeding in getting the initiative on the product delivery roadmap
  • Calculating projected metrics for a business case
  • Cross-collaboration across teams and the organization
  • Understanding backend processes and how they contribute to a better user experience on the front-end, while increasing efficiency for support teams
  • Recognizing the value of service design and its various tenets
  • Lastly, this experience has reaffirmed that sometimes following your instincts really pays off!


As of October 24, 2023, this initiative is in alpha testing, and usability sessions are ongoing. Feedback from these sessions is planned to be incorporated into Phase 2 of the project, scheduled for Q1 2024.

In the initial stages of this initiative, carving out time outside my standard design duties in a newly joined company felt like searching for a needle in a haystack. However, once I found my stride and had the support team lead backing the effort due to her team experiencing the pain of the poor experience on the back end, internal users started sharing their frustrations. The business case organically took shape. There can't be enough said about having stakeholders across the organization support an effort that improves the company, helps them perform their jobs better, and upgrades the experience for end-users—all in one go!
